Dear friends of Taiwan,

October is Taiwan’s Pride Month, and the nation’s largest LGBTQ+ parade will take place on October 25. As the first country in Asia to legalize same-sex marriage, Taiwan continues to move forward on the path toward gender and sexual equality. This is the perfect time to share a few outstanding comics that explore gender and identity in diverse, thought-provoking ways.

When people think of Taiwanese comics, historical themes often come to mind. Yet Taiwan is also a creative powerhouse in the BL (Boys’ Love) and GL (Girls’ Love) genres, fields in which it stands as a leader across Asia. Both commercial and doujin (self-published) works thrive here. Books from Taiwan’s remarkable stories showcase artistic excellence and invite deeper reflection on gender identity, sexual orientation, and the complexities of human desire.

In an era marked by the rise of far-right movements and anti-transgender sentiment worldwide, D.S.’s BLOSSOM offers a story that is both tender and radical. Its protagonist Yufan, a teenager raised as a girl but who discovers she is intersex, struggles to find her place amid clashing family expectations and shifting cultural values. This ultimately hopeful coming-of-age story describes a young person’s pursuit of authenticity, while reminding us that biological sex is a social construct. Meanwhile, Metalmouse’s Embers and Petals, soon to be published in print after its webcomic success, transcends the conventions of vampire romance. Its story of witchhunts and public condemnation is a metaphor for how sexual minorities become branded and isolated.

Wu Shi-shan’s A Two-Faced Couple, a classic BL romance, captures how love can arrive unexpectedly. An office worker who has only dated women is transformed after his colleague kisses him. Challenging the conventions of heterosexual romance, the book portrays love as a mutual and dynamic process. And The New Member, adapted from a musical, stands as an unconventional BL ensemble piece—more a group dance than a duet. Seen through a fujoshi’s eyes, love becomes a stage where male characters can express their feelings freely, while unconventional female classmates script a vivid, alternative vision of an LGBT+ utopia.

BLOSSOM

Written and illustrated by D.S.

#gender #identity #family #comics

* Rights sold: French, Italian

* Full English PDF Available

In Blossom, Yufan has always been told to “act like a girl”: to be quiet, graceful, and obedient. Under the weight of her mother’s and grandmother’s expectations, she suppresses her true self to fit into an idealized image of femininity.

Her world changes when she learns she is intersex—and that her mother once performed a Daoist ritual to ensure a son. As puberty arrives, her confusion deepens; her body and her identity diverge from the norms imposed upon her.

Published shortly before Taiwan legalized same-sex marriage, Blossom is a tender and courageous exploration of gender, family, and belonging. It weaves personal struggle with cultural symbolism, revealing how tradition can both wound and heal. When Yufan joins a queer rights movement and witnesses her cousin’s same-sex wedding at a temple, she finally glimpses a world where love blooms in every color.

Empathetic and deeply moving, Blossom confronts the intersections of gender, faith, and freedom—reminding readers that true beauty lies in authenticity, and that every flower deserves to bloom in its own light.

EMBERS AND PETALS

Written and illustrated by Metalmouse

#vampire #queer #love #webtoon

* Full English PDF Available

In Embers and Petals, a human named Bertha seeks revenge against vampires for her father’s death—until she’s assigned to guard Agnes, a captive vampire. What begins as hostility slowly unfolds into tenderness, as Bertha discovers the truth: the “monsters” her people have been hunting are actually mutated villagers—victims of human experiments.

As Bertha recalls her childhood friendship with Agnes, she learns her father died protecting her. The two flee together, confessing their love even as tragedy looms. Fatally poisoned, Bertha sacrifices herself to save Agnes, who vows to live on. Centuries later, Agnes runs a flower shop, keeping alive Bertha’s dream of a world free from hate.

Empathetic and visually stunning, Embers and Petals transforms the vampire romance tradition. Instead of fetishized bloodlust, it roots queer love in care, memory, and shared ideals—offering an emotionally authentic narrative that transcends genre conventions.

A TWO-FACED COUPLE

Written and illustrated by Wu Shi-shan

#BL #office #romance #comics

* Rights Sold: Italian, German

* Full English PDF Available

In A Two-Faced Couple, Wu You once basked in the spotlight during his youth—but adulthood has dulled his glow. Now an ordinary office worker blending into the background, he lives quietly and alone. After witnessing a touching family reunion on his commute home, he wonders if it’s time to find someone to share his life with.

When his mild-mannered junior colleague, Chiang Kun-fan, confesses his feelings out of the blue, Wu You dismisses it as a joke. But Kun-fan’s sudden intensity—his direct gaze and forceful kiss—shatters Wu You’s composure and ignites a confusing attraction. Their relationship oscillates between warmth and tension, mirroring the contradictions hidden beneath their polite exteriors.

Stylish and emotionally charged, A Two-Faced Couple captures the thrill and vulnerability of unexpected love. Through expressive storytelling, Wu Shi-shan reveals that everyone harbors a dual self: the one they show to others, and the one that trembles when touched by love.

This BL romance draws readers into a dance of restraint and desire, showing how falling in love is both a revelation and a risk.

THE NEW MEMBER

Written by Li-Ying Chien

Illustrated by Ai-Yang

#club #fujoshi #queer #comics

* Full English PDF Available

In The New Member, love, music, and manga collide in a story that celebrates both fujoshi culture and the journey toward marriage equality. Adapted from the first-ever BL rock musical by acclaimed playwright Li-Ying Chien, this comic captures the pure, electric pulse of being seventeen—when friendship, desire, and creativity blur together beneath the stage lights.

At the heart of the story is a fujoshi narrator who invites readers to experience love through her eyes, breaking the boundary between audience and character. Amid laughter, heartbreak, and rooftop jam sessions, the story intertwines two generations: a pair of teachers whose secret love continues to be stifled by social norms, and a new group of students who dare to love freely. “If real life can’t be like manga,” the narrator declares, “then it’s the fault of real life!”

Joyful, self-aware, and deeply moving, The New Member reimagines BL as a space for both queer and fujoshi voices to converge. Through rhythm, rebellion, and empathy, it celebrates love in all its evolving forms—on the stage, in the classroom, and within the hearts of those brave enough to dream.
